# Outreach Templates

Replace `[bracketed]` fields before sending. Lead with the *judgment layer* framing —
never "chatbot," never "AI support." The wedge ICP: **subscription SaaS, $50–500 ARPU,
refund-heavy categories** (devtools, education, fitness apps) on Zendesk + Stripe.

---

## Template A — Warm network (founder / ops you know)

**Subject:** Quick favor — testing a support decision tool

> Hey [Name],
>
> I'm building something for support teams: when a ticket comes in (refund, billing
> dispute, escalation), the system pulls customer history + applies your refund/SLA rules
> and gives the agent a clear recommendation with reasoning — approve, deny, escalate, or
> ask a question.
>
> Not a chatbot. More like a judgment layer so agents don't make inconsistent calls.
>
> I'm looking for **one team** to run a **free 30-day pilot** on real tickets. I'd handle
> setup; I'd need honest feedback and, if it works, a short testimonial.
>
> Worth 15 minutes this week to see if it fits [Company]?

---

## Template B — Cold LinkedIn (Head of Support / COO)

**Connection note (≤300 chars):**

> Building a tool that recommends refund/escalate/deny on every support ticket with
> plain-English reasoning + full audit log. 2-week Zendesk plug-in. Would a 15-min demo be
> useful for [Company]'s team?

**Follow-up DM (after connect):**

> Thanks for connecting, [Name].
>
> Quick context: most support teams decide refunds/escalations from gut feel + tribal
> knowledge. We sit between the ticket and the agent — pull Stripe/history, apply your
> policies, recommend an action with confidence + reason.
>
> Pilots are **free for 30 days**. Setup is on us. Typical fit: SaaS teams doing 200+
> judgment-call tickets/month on Zendesk + Stripe.
>
> Open to a 15-min screen share? I can show a live ticket flow in under 10 minutes.

## Template C — Post-demo, no decision yet

> Hi [Name] — thanks again for the demo.
>
> Three things teams usually care about before a pilot:
> 1. **Wrong refund risk** — every decision is logged; policy rules run before any AI
>    suggestion, and the AI can't choose an action policy doesn't allow.
> 2. **Agent adoption** — one-click approve/override inside the queue.
> 3. **ROI** — [X] judgment tickets/day × [Y] min saved × [Z] cost/hour.
>
> If helpful, I can run a **2-week pilot on your Zendesk sandbox or a tagged queue** — no
> commitment, no card.
>
> Does [date] work for a 20-min kickoff?

## Template E — Finance / CFO angle (when ops loops them in)

**Subject:** Audit trail for support refund decisions

> Hi [Name],
>
> [Ops contact] asked me to share how we handle refund judgment calls on support tickets.
>
> For every ticket: customer LTV, prior refunds, policy rule hits, AI recommendation,
> agent action, and override reason — stored in an audit log exportable for finance review.
>
> Goal: fewer inconsistent refunds, faster resolution, full traceability.
>
> Happy to join a 15-min call or send a one-page security + data flow summary.

## Sequencing

1. **Warm first.** One founder/ops head you know → free pilot → testimonial.
2. **Cold with proof.** 5 targeted LinkedIn DMs/week to Head of Support / COO at ICP SaaS.
3. **Referral after every pilot.** One warm intro beats 100 cold emails.

Pair every send with the [demo script](DEMO_SCRIPT.md) and the
[security one-pager](SECURITY.md).
